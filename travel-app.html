Via Travel App

Figma · Marvel App · Illustrator · Photoshop

Project Overview

Create a mobile app that allows users to book flights, a hotel, and a car on specific dates to different destinations. The travel app will focus on helping users to organize their plans into one itinerary.

Goals

  • Help users book a flight, hotel, and car on a specific date for different destinations.
  • Assist in organizing all travel plans into one itinerary.
  • Ability to learn about local experiences and culture.

Challenges

  • Balancing the scope of how many solutions one app can solve.
  • Finding a need within the travel industry which is not being met.
  • Helping users feel more comfortable traveling.

Solutions

  • Allow users to freely explore locations before traveling.
  • Assist in planning trips through an easy to use interface where they can view their saved options.
  • Make planning a less tedious exeperience that can be done entirely on the app.

Process

Research

Mind Mapping

Travel is a very broad subject with many different factors to consider and there are so many potential issues that travelers can face between planning their trip, visiting the destination, and returning home.

This makes it difficult to create one app that can do it all and we will need to narrow down the scope of what we can do to help users. Using a mind map I divided the travel process into clear sections to explore further.

User Interviews

Using the mind map, I was able to narrow down topics from each section that I wanted to learn more about through user interviews. These included:

  • How users like to plan their trips?
  • How they choose what activities to do at their destination?
  • How they like to document their travel experiences?
  • How much time goes into a trip from beginning to end?

I then conducted 6 interviews with experienced travelers where I was able to get 4 main takeaways:

  • Planning trips is a very time consuming process. You are forced to search across multiple websites and apps to find all the information you need about flights and the locations you will travel to.
  • Travelers like to receive tips on where to go and what to do. They want to learn what places would be most interesting to them before arriving and like to see that other travelers had good experiences there.
  • Travelers like to share their experiences and document their trips through photos and video on their social media accounts.
  • Since there are so many steps in the travel process, they often feel stressed that something will go wrong and one mistake could throw off the entire trip.

User Personas

Analyzing the pain points my interviewed users brought up. One point that was agreed upon unanimously was how annoying the process of finding all the information they need to plan a trip was. The information is scattered across many different sources on the web, which leads to far too many hours spent organizing that info.

I created a few user personas to reference their pain points.

Empathy Map

Finding a solution that makes travel planning easier has revealed itself to be the most important user need. While the only help they need with documenting their trips is finding the right locations to begin with.

With this in mind I created an empathy map with the results of my interviews to assist with understanding and visualizing user needs in relation to planning for trips.

Defining the main problems we can now try to solve:

  • Help users book a flight, hotel, and car on a specific date for different destinations.
  • Assist in organizing all travel plans into one itinerary.
  • Ability to learn more about local experiences and culture before arriving.

Ideation

User Flow

To start putting together design ideas, I created a user flow for the first iteration of the app. This will show how a user would go through the app to find flights, hotels, cars, and how they would learn more about specific locations.

One thing that stands out to me immediately after creating the user flow is that it would be helpful to add a step after purchasing flights. This would allow you to jump straight to hotels located near your destination airport, rather than going back to the home page and repeating the same search steps.

Low-Fi Wireframes

I then made low-fidelity wireframes to get a better sense of the layout and how each screen would look.

User Feedback

I invited the previously interviewed users back to critique the initial wire flow design. The goal of this was to help continue to make the app more user friendly. The key takeaways were:

  • A place to save locations and hotels users are interested in so they can easily organize the information and review later.
  • Need a profile page option so users can edit their account and view other important information.
  • Users felt there was an opportunity to add more social options so they can share information with friends, especially if they are planning trips together.
  • Allow users to go back and easily edit any entered information.
  • Better integrate the explore section of the app so it feels more connected to searching flights, hotels, and cars on the home page.

Evaluation

Usability Testing

With the prototype made I am now running usability testing to see how well the main goals have been met. In these tests I am inviting users to interact with the prototype having 2 goals in mind:

  • How easily were you able to start the process of booking a flight, hotel, and car?
  • What questions would you have for the app at the beginning stages of planning a trip?

I feel that the planning process within the app can continue to be improved and needs to offer as many options as possible. Is it enough to organize everything in a list? Or should we be doing something more interactive and customizable? Whether more simple or more complex is the answer, this will only be learned after usability testing.

Development & Final Thoughts

Moving forward there are many areas of the app I would like to continue working on. The process of planning trips and expanding on social interactions between friends are 2 key areas I would like to address first. Besides that, there will also be continued UI development and branding.

Overall this project has continued to show me the importance of empathizing with users and involving them in as many steps as possible. Inviting them back to review my intial low-fi wireframes in the ideation phase really changed a lot of design elements for the better. Such as making the home page of the app more user friendly compared to the original 2 page travel and explore format.
